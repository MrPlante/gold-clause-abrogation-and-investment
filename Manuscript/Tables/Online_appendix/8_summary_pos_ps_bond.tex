Long-term liabilities (LTL) are the sum of corporate bonds, preferred shares, and bank debt. The variable $\tilde{d}$ is defined as gold clause bonds divided by the sum of preferred equity and bonds in 1930. In Panels A and B, $\tilde{d}$ can be slightly greater than Corp.\ bonds/LTL because $\tilde{d}$ uses the reported amount outstanding of each bond, whereas Corp.\ bonds/LTL is based on balance sheet information. In Panel C, $\tilde{d}$ can differ significantly from Corp.\ bonds/LTL because $\tilde{d}$ is frozen at each firm's 1930 value while Corp.\ bonds/LTL is contemporaneous.
