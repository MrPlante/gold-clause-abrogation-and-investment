\scriptsize{\textit{Notes.} This table tests the robustness of the dividend results to sample restrictions and alternative dependent variable definitions. It reports results from panel regressions of dividends on gold clause exposure $\tilde{d}$, and period $\times$ $\tilde{d}$ interactions, where the pre-abrogation period (1926--1932) is the omitted category. Columns 1--4 use cash dividends normalized by fixed capital in 1930 as the dependent variable: column 1 (2) includes firm-year observations where a firm paid a positive (zero) amount of cash dividends in the previous year; column 3 (4) includes firms that paid a positive (zero) amount of cash dividends in 1932. Columns 5--8 use the full sample with alternative dependent variable definitions: column 5 uses annual dividend growth (set to zero if dividends are zero in both years); column 6 uses dividends divided by lagged book equity; column 7 uses dividends divided by market capitalization in 1930; column 8 uses dividends as a share of net income for firms with positive net income exceeding the dividend. All regressions include firm and year fixed effects. All variables are winsorized at the 0.5\% and 99.5\% levels within each year. Standard errors in parentheses are two-way clustered by firm and year. $^{*}p<0.10$, $^{**}p<0.05$, $^{***}p<0.01$.}
